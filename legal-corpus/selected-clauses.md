# Selected Clause Corpus

This corpus is intentionally small. It selects representative Chinese AIGC governance provisions that are directly relevant to synthetic actors, digital humans, and generated-content services. The clauses below are short English working summaries for research annotation, not official translations and not legal advice.

## GENAI-2023 Article 4: Content Safety

Providers of generative AI services should comply with law, respect social morality and ethics, and take effective measures to prevent illegal or harmful generated content.

## GENAI-2023 Article 17: Complaint Mechanism

Generative AI service providers should establish complaint and reporting mechanisms, disclose handling procedures, and process complaints in a timely manner.

## DEEPSYN-2023 Article 14: Deep Synthesis Labeling

Deep synthesis service providers should add labels where generated or edited information content may cause public confusion or misidentification.

## LABEL-2025 Article 4: Explicit AIGC Labeling

AI-generated synthetic content provided to the public should carry explicit labels where required by the labeling rules.

## LABEL-2025 Article 5: Implicit or Metadata Labeling

AI-generated synthetic content should carry implicit labels, metadata, or comparable machine-readable identifiers where required.

## GB45438-2025 Section 6: Metadata Label Method

Generated synthetic-content files should include required machine-readable label fields using the specified technical method where applicable.

## PIPL-2021 Article 29: Separate Consent for Sensitive Personal Information

Processing sensitive personal information requires separate consent unless another lawful basis applies.

## PIPL-2021 Article 28: Biometric and Sensitive Personal Information

Biometric identifiers and other sensitive personal information require specific protection because misuse may harm personal dignity or personal/property safety.

## GENAI-2023 Article 7: Model and Service Governance

Providers should lawfully manage training data, model services, and provider obligations, including required filing or disclosure where applicable.
