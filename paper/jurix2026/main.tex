\begin{abstract}
Turning legal provisions into executable controls is not simply a matter of converting prose into fields. Legal duties depend on scope, exceptions, and judgments that may not be reducible to a Boolean test. This article develops a human-reviewed method for translating selected Chinese AIGC provisions into machine-readable controls while keeping the interpretive choices visible. The prototype represents five source instruments as 11 canonical norm artifacts and 12 scoped controls. It separates applicability, evidence testing, and final decision, and records whether a control reflects a direct legal duty, an internal assurance practice, or a technical implementation requirement. Validation covers schemas, source-excerpt integrity, cross-file consistency, runtime evidence, and 21 designed cases; 46 automated tests passed in the evaluated repository state. These results show that reviewed interpretations can be executed and routed consistently. They do not show that the interpretations are legally correct or that any real service is compliant. The claim is narrower: software may execute a reviewed legal interpretation, but it should not be treated as the interpreter.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Moving from legal text to an engineering rule requires more than a change of format. Statutes identify actors, duties, triggers, exceptions, and qualitative standards; control engines need fields, expressions, and outcomes. Open-textured and defeasible rules resist direct Boolean representation \cite{prakken1997logical}. In China-facing AIGC services, the gap is concrete. Metadata presence may be observable, but label prominence, filing applicability, and the scope of a consent-related duty call for legal judgment.

In building the prototype, the main difficulty was not writing an evaluator. It was deciding where legal interpretation ended and engineering implementation began. A test could be syntactically valid and still claim more than its source justified. The question guiding this study is therefore upstream of execution: \emph{Where did the control come from, and can its derivation remain reviewable?}

The study contributes a source-to-control method for a small Chinese AIGC corpus. It also separates three decisions often collapsed into one automation label: whether a rule applies, whether its evidence can be tested, and whether the machine result may be final. The prototype records these choices in 11 norm artifacts, 12 scoped controls, and 21 designed evaluation cases. The aim is not automated legal interpretation. It is a reviewable account of the interpretation that the software executes.

\section{Related Work}

These strands of work solve different parts of the problem. Model Cards organize disclosure about AI models \cite{mitchell2019model}, and the AI RMF supplies a risk-governance vocabulary \cite{nistAiRmf2023}. OSCAL goes further by representing controls and assessment artifacts in machine-readable form \cite{nistOscal}. Work on machine-readable AI compliance evidence then shows how assurance records can be generated from such controls \cite{ugarte2026evidence}. In each case, however, the relevant control is largely assumed to exist already.

Rules as Code turns attention toward the rulemaking process itself \cite{mohun2020cracking}. The British Nationality Act logic program remains a landmark example of executable legislation \cite{sergot1986nationality}, while LegalRuleML offers a standard representation for legal rules \cite{legalRuleMl2021}. These approaches make legal structure computable, but they do not remove questions of exception, priority, or interpretation.

LN2FR treats the translation of concrete legal texts into formal representations as a continuing methodological challenge \cite{borges2023ln2fr}. Related studies examine legal alignment in legislation encoding \cite{witt2024encoding}, formal compliance checking \cite{francesconi2023patterns,calimeri2023compliance}, and the specification of an entire statute for decision support \cite{doesburg2019aliens}. The unresolved step addressed here is narrower: recording the choices made between a Chinese clause and the control eventually executed by an evaluator.

\section{Transformation Framework}

\subsection{Corpus and Representation}

I limited the corpus to five instruments that repeatedly arise in synthetic-actor and digital-human work: the Interim Measures for Generative AI Services \cite{genaiMeasures2023}, the Deep Synthesis Provisions \cite{deepSynthesisProvisions2022}, the AIGC Labeling Measures \cite{aigcLabelingMeasures2025}, GB 45438--2025 \cite{gb45438}, and the Personal Information Protection Law \cite{pipl2021}. This was a practical choice, not a claim about the boundaries of Chinese AI law. A provision was included only when its source could be traced and its actor, duty, trigger, exception, evidence need, and review status could be recorded without inventing missing detail.

Each provision becomes a canonical norm artifact rather than a free-form summary. Thirteen elements are recorded: source, normative fragment, actor, modality, action, object, trigger, exception, timing, evidence requirement, ambiguity, automation profile, and review status. The source record includes the Chinese excerpt, an unofficial working translation, an official URL, effective-date metadata, and a SHA-256 digest. That digest can reveal a change to the repository excerpt. It cannot verify the excerpt against the current official webpage.

\subsection{Clause-to-Control Pipeline}

Keeping the two apart matters when one norm produces several controls or when an internal assurance practice goes beyond the wording of the duty \cite{witt2024encoding}.

An early version treated Deep Synthesis Article~14 as a single consent control. That reading was too broad. The provision directly supports a provider-prompt duty, whereas retaining proof of the edited person's consent is better described as a derived organizational assurance. The two controls were therefore separated \cite{deepSynthesisProvisions2022}.

Runtime evidence is validated independently of the control definition. Its records may contain business facts, human confirmations, and keyed reviews. A malformed record is therefore distinguishable from a complete record whose facts remain uncertain or conflicting.

\subsection{Automation and Review Boundaries}

A single automation label proved misleading during development. Some controls had deterministic evidence tests but still depended on a human decision about applicability. Others produced a machine result that could not become final without review. The control model now records applicability, evidence testing, and final decision separately.

Control derivation is recorded separately as a direct legal requirement, a derived organizational assurance, or a technical implementation control. The labels do not change how an expression runs. They state what the expression is entitled to claim about its legal source.

The Article~9 exception also required a narrower reading than the first implementation assumed. Evidence supporting delivery of unlabelled content to a requesting user does not settle what happens when that content is later published or redistributed. The control was therefore limited to the provider-delivery stage. Within that scope, label existence is deterministic after a reviewer confirms that the explicit-label scenario applies \cite{aigcLabelingMeasures2025}.

At repository commit \texttt{9694fb3}, schema checks covered all 11 norm artifacts and 12 controls, and the source-excerpt and cross-file checks reported no errors. All 21 designed cases followed their declared routes; 46 automated regression and integrity tests passed.

The adverse cases preserve mistakes encountered while refining the prototype. An ordinary Boolean was initially capable of standing in for a human confirmation, the Article~9 exception was too broadly scoped, and an aggregate metadata flag could conceal a missing required field. The final tests keep those cases as regression checks. Within this repository state, the artifacts are internally consistent and the evaluator follows the declared review routes. That is the extent of the result. The experiment does not determine whether the underlying legal interpretations are correct.

\section{Discussion and Limitations}

The development process showed why the term ``automated'' needs qualification. A deterministic test says little about who decided that the test was legally applicable. Treating those questions as the same would make an output look more certain than its interpretation permits. The same caution applies to direct duties and derived assurances: useful internal evidence practices should not be reported as statutory language.

The most difficult controls were not those with the largest number of fields. They were built around terms such as prominent label, effective measures, and public-opinion or social-mobilization capacity \cite{aigcLabelingMeasures2025,deepSynthesisProvisions2022,genaiMeasures2023}. Downstream evidence can sometimes be checked, but the legal threshold remains open to judgment. Machine-readable rules execute a recorded interpretation; they do not become the interpreter. Likewise, a source-excerpt hash verifies repository integrity, not fidelity to the current official text.

Independent review has not been completed. A second coder is planned for all 11 norms, followed by qualified legal review of source fidelity, derivation choices, the Article~9 scope, Deep Synthesis Article~14, filing applicability, biometric classification, and open-textured concepts. Disagreements will remain in versioned adjudication records rather than being overwritten.

The corpus covers five instruments, and one author made the initial interpretations. The English translations are working translations, not official ones. The cases were designed for method validation, and the evaluator is not a production legal-judgment system. Law and regulatory interpretation may also change. Nothing reported here constitutes legal advice or compliance certification.

\section{Conclusion}

This study examined the step between a legal provision and the control eventually executed by a compliance tool. The prototype shows that source text, interpretive choices, evidence tests, and review boundaries can remain connected. Some checks are deterministic. The harder questions---whether a legal trigger applies and whether qualitative evidence is sufficient---remain with human reviewers. The current results concern internal consistency and evaluation routing, not legal validity. The next stage is independent coding and qualified legal review, including transparent, versioned revision of interpretations that do not survive that scrutiny.
